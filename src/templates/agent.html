Negotiation model

The agent will negotiate on your behalf within some default budgets.

There are a few schemes with which you can specify your negotiation model.

Scheme 1: Distributions

You can specify the minimum and maximum margins around market price. You can also specify the distribution of your offers within this range.

For example, if your margins are [90, 105], and your distribution is [0,10, 20, 40, 80, 100], and the average market price is 100k, the offers will be [90k, 91.5k, 93k, 96k, 102k, 105k].

You can configure how many offers the agent makes, by adding more points in the distribution.

This scheme is most flexible and it is going to be your default model, as it is not tied to any market price specifically.

Scheme 2: Margins

You specify the margins around market price as a percentage.

For example, if you margins are [90, 92, 97, 99, 100], then,

  • First offer: 90% of market price
  • Second offer: 92% of market price
  • Third offer: 97% of market price, and so on

Scheme 3: Prices (explicit)

You specify the exact prices that will be used during negotiation.

With this scheme, you can control exactly how the negotiation proceeds.

You can use scheme 2 and 3 on a listing-basis only, as offers made in one listing might not apply for another listing.

Default negotiation model:

Your default scheme has to be distributions, as it doesn't require you to specify any particular price.

Not all listings are the same. For example, you might request for X quantity of materials in Y time. If the time is too short for the requested quantity, the sellers might ask for a higher price.

You must be willing to pay a higher price, otherwise, your offers won't be accepted because the deadlines are too tight.

Clarity about ZOPA:

Every quote from the buyer which is less than the seller's least offer, is unacceptable for the seller.

Every quote from the seller which is greater than the buyer's highest offer, is unacceptable for the buyer.

The offers and counter-offers that are made are public, visible only to relevant actors. The calculations and reasoning behind the values will be private, visible only to you.

Bidding model

Here you can set up auction increments.

In an auction listing, the agent will place a bid automatically, if permitted.

After receiving information about a new bid, the agent will try to outbid the current highest bid by adding this increment to the current highest bid, and placing a new bid.

This process will continue till the agent runs out of budget.
